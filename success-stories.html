Jennifer Parker

Class of 2015

B.S. Computer Science

Google

San Francisco, CA

From Campus to Google: My Journey as a Software Engineer

Technology Career Growth

After graduating in 2015, I joined Google as a junior software engineer. The alumni network played a crucial role in my career journey. Through connections I made on this platform, I received mentorship from senior alumni already working at Google.

What started as an internship referral turned into a full-time position. The technical skills I gained at university provided a strong foundation, but it was the soft skills and networking opportunities that truly accelerated my career.

Five years later, I'm now a senior engineer leading a team of my own. I frequently return to campus to recruit new talent and mentor current students, paying forward the support I received.

Michael Johnson

Class of 2012

MBA

Microsoft

Seattle, WA

Building Products That Impact Millions

Technology Product Management

My journey from business student to Product Manager at Microsoft was anything but linear. After completing my MBA in 2012, I worked in several roles before finding my passion in product management.

The turning point came when I connected with an alumni mentor through this platform who worked at Microsoft. Their guidance helped me navigate the interview process and understand what it takes to succeed in tech product management.

Now, I lead a team responsible for features used by millions of people worldwide. The problem-solving skills I developed during my university years continue to serve me well in my daily work.

Sarah Williams

Class of 2018

M.S. Data Science

Amazon

New York, NY

From Research to Real-World Data Solutions

Technology Data Science

After completing my Master's in Data Science, I joined Amazon as a data scientist. My graduate research project caught the attention of an Amazon recruiter who reached out through the alumni network.

At Amazon, I work on developing machine learning models that improve customer experiences. The rigorous research training I received at university prepared me for tackling complex, ambiguous problems.

I'm grateful for the alumni connections that helped me secure this position. Now I actively participate in campus recruiting events and mentor students interested in data science careers.

Robert Chen

Class of 2010

B.S. Electrical Engineering

Tesla

Austin, TX

Engineering the Future of Transportation

Engineering Innovation

As a senior engineer at Tesla, I work on cutting-edge battery technology that powers the future of transportation. My journey began in the electrical engineering labs at university, where I developed a passion for sustainable energy solutions.

After graduation, I worked at several automotive companies before landing my dream job at Tesla. An alumni connection referred me to the hiring manager, which gave my application a significant boost.

The technical foundation I built at university, combined with the professional network I developed through alumni events, has been instrumental in my career success. I frequently collaborate with university researchers on new projects.
